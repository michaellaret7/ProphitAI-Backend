Require the stock to have traded normally in the prior session (skip halted or unusual-volume names). Optionable names preferred (liquid options signal high-quality institutional activity, tighter spreads, and deeper two-sided flow in opening hour). Target pre-signal universe approximately 200 to 400 names, from which the top and bottom deciles give approximately 20 to 40 names per leg. Universe is naturally smaller than daily-rebalance strategies because 15-minute-bar intraday signal-to-noise requires above-average liquidity concentration; 200 names at 50 million USD ADV is a sweet spot balancing cross-sectional breadth against execution quality.

### Entry & Exit
Daily intraday cycle. Entry at 10:30 ET each trading day (after four 15-minute bars from session open); exit at 15:45 ET (15 minutes before market close to avoid closing-auction impact). Signal: for each universe name, compute opening-range return as the price at 10:30 bar close minus the price at 9:30 bar open divided by the price at 9:30 bar open. Rank universe cross-sectionally on this single variable. Long entry: top decile (approximately top 10 percent, roughly 20 to 40 names depending on universe size). Short entry: bottom decile (approximately bottom 10 percent, same count). Positions are equal-weighted within each leg and sized so long gross equals short gross (dollar-neutral). Enter all positions at 10:30 bar close using limit orders at prevailing mid or slightly aggressive marketable limits; if a name does not fill within the 10:30 to 10:45 window, skip it and the portfolio runs slightly smaller that day. Hold positions unchanged through the 10:30 to 15:45 intraday window; no intraday rebalancing. Exit triggers: (a) primary scheduled exit at 15:45 bar close every day, mandatory (all positions flat by market close; strategy holds zero overnight risk), (b) intraday stop-out if adverse move exceeds approximately 2 times the trailing 20-day ATR of the name (single hard stop per position to cap tail risk from intraday news or unexpected halts), (c) intraday halt-exit: if a market-wide circuit breaker triggers or if the individual name is halted for news, close all related positions at the next available print. Skip-day rules: do not trade on days with scheduled FOMC announcements or Fed Chair press conferences during the hold window; also skip days with CPI or NFP releases if the release time falls within the 9:30 to 10:30 opening range window (major releases at 8:30 ET pre-market are fine; FOMC 14:00 releases disrupt the afternoon hold window and should be skip days); also skip the first trading day after a major market halt. Rebalancing frequency: every trading day on a fresh signal (approximately 250 entry days per year, approximately 250 exit days per year). Expected one-way turnover approximately 5000 to 10000 percent per leg annualized (every position opened and closed daily). All data inputs are price-only: 15-minute OHLC bars on each universe name and daily ATR for the stop rule. No external data sources required (no SPY, no VIX, no macro series, no fundamentals, no earnings calendar beyond a macro-release skip-day list and a pre-earnings skip for names reporting that session).

### Risk Management
Position sizing: equal-weight within each leg with approximately 20 to 40 names per leg, giving per-name weight of approximately 2.5 to 5 percent of leg gross. Target dollar-neutral (long gross equals short gross) with tolerance approximately plus or minus 5 percent. Portfolio gross exposure sized so total intraday portfolio volatility targets approximately 10 to 15 percent annualized using trailing 60-session realized portfolio return standard deviation (Barroso-Santa-Clara 2015 style constant-vol overlay on portfolio-own intraday returns); gross capped at approximately 200 percent (roughly 100 long 100 short) and floored at approximately 50 percent. Vol overlay uses only portfolio-own daily P and L series, zero external data. Sector concentration cap: maximum approximately 30 percent gross per GICS sector on each leg (prevents intraday sector concentration when one sector dominates early-session moves, for example an AI-news day where all tech rallies). Per-name cap approximately 5 percent of leg gross. Intraday tail stop at approximately 2 times trailing 20-day ATR adverse move per position (caps single-name blowup). Daily drawdown circuit breaker: if the strategy realizes a single-day loss worse than approximately negative 3 percent of gross, halt trading for 2 business days before resuming. Rolling 20-session drawdown guardrail: if trailing 20-session return is worse than approximately negative 8 percent, halve gross exposure for 10 trading days before restoring. Short-leg borrow gate: exclude names flagged hard-to-borrow and those with implied borrow cost above roughly 100 basis points annualized; because short positions are held only 5.5 hours intraday, borrow cost impact per trade is minimal but locate availability is essential. Primary tail risks to monitor: (1) intraday factor-flip days (DeepSeek January 2025 style, AI-selloff days) where first-hour winners suddenly become afternoon losers as news hits, mitigated by 2-ATR stop and sector cap, (2) market-wide volatility shocks during the hold window (tariff headlines, geopolitical news hitting at 12 or 13 ET), mitigated by skip-day rules around scheduled macro events and the 3 percent daily circuit breaker, (3) earnings events clustering (many names in top or bottom decile may be reporting after-hours), mitigated by skipping any name with scheduled earnings release that day, (4) short-squeeze risk on the short leg, mitigated by the 2-ATR stop and the 5.5-hour hold (short squeezes typically develop over days, less within-session), (5) opening-auction dislocation events, mitigated by using 10:30 entry rather than 9:30 (by 10:30 the opening auction imbalances have typically cleared).

### Research Backing
Heston S, Korajczyk R, Sadka R (2010) "Intraday Patterns in the Cross-Section of Stock Returns" Journal of Finance 65(4) pages 1369 to 1407 — half-hour returns exhibit strong same-time-of-day persistence across US equities for at least 40 subsequent trading days driven by predictable institutional execution patterns; foundational paper for intraday cross-sectional momentum. Gao L, Han Y, Li S, Zhou G (2018) "Market Intraday Momentum" Journal of Financial Economics 129(2) — first half-hour return predicts last half-hour return in S and P 500 index with t-stats of 3 to 5; Sharpe of the timing strategy exceeds 1 at monthly frequency; robust 1993 to 2013 and out-of-sample; mechanism attributed to late-informed-trader behavior. Lou D, Polk C, Skouras S (2019) "A Tug of War: Overnight Versus Intraday Expected Returns" — documents firm-level continuation in intraday returns (open to close) and offsetting cross-period reversal with overnight returns; different clienteles (intermediaries intraday, individuals overnight) drive the patterns; overnight-intraday spread forecasts strategy time variation. Moskowitz T, Ooi Y, Pedersen L (2012) "Time Series Momentum" Journal of Financial Economics 104 — own-asset return continuation at multiple horizons including short-horizon intraday; cross-sectional extension implied. Jegadeesh N, Titman S (1993) "Returns to Buying Winners and Selling Losers" Journal of Finance 48 — foundational cross-sectional momentum principle, extended to intraday horizon here. Barroso P, Santa-Clara P (2015) "Momentum Has Its Moments" Journal of Financial Economics 116 — constant-vol overlay on portfolio-own realized vol nearly doubles momentum Sharpe and cuts kurtosis from 18.24 to 2.68; applied here on daily P and L series. Khandani A, Lo A (2007 and 2011) — short-horizon reversal and quant-crisis literature establishes that high-turnover intraday strategies are feasible on liquid US equities with disciplined execution; the August 2007 quant crisis was a daily-horizon momentum unwind, not an intraday one, so intraday strategies held minimal exposure to that event. Real-world opening-range breakout implementations on SPY demonstrate net-of-cost annualized returns in the high teens from 2007 to early 2024 with documented commission and slippage treatment (Interactive Brokers 0.0035 USD per share pricing, limit orders, VWAP-linked execution), confirming the edge survives realistic intraday transaction costs on liquid names. Current April 2026 regime evidence: VIX closed 19.5 on 2026-04-21 after dropping from a 31.05 spike on 2026-03-27 to a recent range of 17 to 19 (moderate vol regime supportive of cross-sectional momentum per Daniel-Moskowitz 2016); Goldman Sachs Flows Desk 2026-01-21 documents SPX single-stock dispersion at the 97th percentile on 4-year lookback (peak stock-picker regime with maximum cross-sectional opportunity); JPMorgan Fixed Income Weekly April 2026 documents that intraday 2-look realized volatility is above close-to-close realized volatility, confirming intraday moves are the dominant component of current price discovery driven by newsflow sensitivity and high-frequency policy uncertainty; JPMorgan 2026-02-06 places VIX at the borderline between middle and high equity vol regime (favorable for vol-scaled intraday strategies). Disconfirming evidence: momentum factor ETFs such as MTUM have underperformed in narrow-market regimes (2024 AI concentration) because daily 12-1 cross-sectional rank is heavily crowded; intraday opening-range rank is NOT part of the ETF complex and faces materially less crowding as of April 2026. Per Lou-Polk-Skouras 2019 some reversal strategies lose all profit overnight while others lose it intraday; this strategy is constructed to stay entirely in the intraday window (10:30 to 15:45 ET) and never hold overnight risk, so the tug-of-war works for rather than against the construction. Execution realism: with approximately 20 to 40 names per leg and strict liquidity gates (50 million USD ADV, 10 USD price minimum), typical per-name trade size of approximately 0.05 to 0.2 percent of daily volume is achievable with limit-order execution in the first 15 minutes after 10:30, keeping slippage within roughly 5 to 10 basis points per round trip.
