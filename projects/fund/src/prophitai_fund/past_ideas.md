### Description
A fully intraday, 1-minute bar strategy that exploits three compounding anomalies: (1) intraday time-of-day seasonality (post-open 9:35–10:30 and post-lunch 12:00–13:30 are structurally biased upward per Hirsch 1987 and HMM research); (2) volume-clock adjustment of intraday returns (bars with above-average volume for their time-of-day slot contain more genuine price discovery, while low-volume bars are noise); and (3) cross-sectional sector-relative ranking of volume-adjusted returns within GICS sectors. The strategy constructs a Volume-Adjusted Intraday Return (VAIR) by multiplying each 1-minute return by the bar's Volume Impulse Ratio (current bar volume / rolling 20-session average for that same time-of-day slot, capped at 3×). It then ranks stocks cross-sectionally within sectors by their rolling 30-minute VAIR cumulation score, entering longs in top-quintile relative outperformers and shorts in bottom-quintile relative underperformers during seasonally favorable windows. All positions are closed intraday (30-minute max hold, mandatory flat before 15:35 PM). NO overnight positions. This is entirely distinct from the VSIGM strategy which uses overnight gaps held for 1–5 days.

### Edge
Volume Impulse Ratio (VIR) = V_today(i,s) / V_avg(i,s) over rolling 20 sessions for each same-time slot s. VAIR(i,t) = raw_1min_return(i,t) × min(VIR(i,t), 3.0). Rolling 30-min VAIR_Momentum = cumulative sum of VAIR over 30 bars. Relative Stock Score = VAIR_Momentum(individual) - cap-weighted sector mean VAIR_Momentum. Enter during TOD windows: 9:35–10:30 AM and 12:00–13:30 PM. Long top quintile relative score + positive sector momentum. Short bottom quintile + negative sector momentum. VWAP Gate: price must be within ±0.3% of session VWAP at entry (prevents chasing extended moves).

### Universe
US equities only. Market cap > $5B. 20-day avg daily dollar volume > $100M. 20-day avg daily share volume > 500K. Stock price > $20 (bid-ask spread proxy). S&P 500 or Russell 1000 membership. Minimum 3-month trading history (for 20-session TOD baseline). Exclude: ADRs, SPACs, leveraged ETFs, closed-end funds.

### Entry & Exit
ENTRY (each 1-minute bar during active windows): Long if Relative Stock Score > 80th pctile within sector AND Sector VAIR Momentum > 0 AND price within ±0.3% of VWAP. Short if Relative Stock Score < 20th pctile within sector AND Sector VAIR Momentum < 0 AND price within ±0.3% of VWAP. ACTIVE WINDOWS: 9:35–10:30 AM and 12:00–13:30 PM ET only. EXIT: E1: Relative Stock Score crosses zero. E2: Price deviates > 0.5% from session VWAP in entry direction (position extended). E3: Hard time stop — 30 minutes from entry. E4: Sector VAIR Momentum reverses sign. E5: Mandatory close of all positions by 15:35 PM. INACTIVE PERIODS: 9:30–9:35 (opening volatility), 10:30–12:00 (midday lull), 13:30–15:30 (afternoon drift), 15:30–16:00 (leveraged ETF rebalancing noise).

### Risk Management
No overnight positions (hard rule). Single-name max: 10% of daily gross exposure. Sector max: 30% of gross exposure. VIX sizing: VIX < 18 → 100% target size; VIX 18–25 → 75%; VIX 25–35 → 50%; VIX > 35 → halt new entries, close existing. Daily drawdown stop: realized P&L < -1.5% NAV → halt all new entries rest of day. Per-position stop-loss: 0.4% adverse move from entry. Current regime (VIX ~22): Operate at 75% sizing with 0.25% tight stop.

### Description
A 15-minute bar intraday momentum strategy that tracks the directional footprint of institutional meta-order execution in US large-cap equities. The core thesis: large institutional orders are split over 30-90 minute VWAP/TWAP windows, leaving a detectable autocorrelated signature in bar-level price-volume data. By constructing an Order Flow Imbalance (OFI) proxy from OHLCV data — OFI_proxy = Volume × (Close − Low)/(High − Low) — and normalizing it relative to the same time-of-day's rolling 20-day history, the strategy identifies bars where directional buying/selling pressure significantly exceeds its typical level. The Opening Range (first 30 minutes) establishes which direction the institutional intent is pointed; sustained above-average OFI for 2+ consecutive bars confirms the meta-order is ongoing. This operates at 15-min resolution specifically because: (a) it is above the microstructure noise floor that killed the 1-min IVCCM strategy; (b) 15 min is precisely the mid-point of a typical 60-90 min institutional execution window; and (c) 15-min aggregation acts as a low-pass filter against the headline-driven noise prevalent in the April 2026 macro regime. The strategy is architecturally distinct from IVCCM: it uses OFI directional signed volume (not VIR ratio), an opening-range directional gate (not VWAP gate), HMM-inspired vol conditioning (not cross-sectional sector ranking), and a 15-min bar (not 1-min).

### Edge
Core signal: OFI_proxy_i,t = Volume_i,t × (Close_i,t − Low_i,t) / (High_i,t − Low_i,t). This is a Kyle-Lambda-style estimate of net buying pressure constructed purely from OHLCV. Cross-sectionally normalized as a z-score against the same time-of-day's rolling 20-day baseline. Three compounding signals: (1) OFI Proxy z-score — primary alpha signal; (2) Opening Range Gate (Dual Thrust: BuyLine = OR_High + K1 × Range, SellLine = OR_Low − K2 × Range) — directional anchor, ensures OFI signal is aligned with early institutional direction; (3) OFI Persistence Filter — both current and prior bar must show above-median z-scores in the same direction, confirming sustained flow rather than single-bar noise. Supporting conditioning: bar-level realized vol ratio (0.5× to 2.5× rolling avg) screens out panic vol and dead vol bars; cross-sectional OFI rank must be top tercile within universe for entry. Mechanism: Hawkes process self-excitation → trade-sign autocorrelation persists at 15-min aggregation; institutional VWAP/TWAP algorithms fragment meta-orders into identifiable sub-orders; Lee & Swaminathan (2000) confirms momentum is strongest in high-volume stocks.

### Universe
US equities only. Market cap >= $10B. 20-day avg daily dollar volume >= $150M. Stock price >= $15. S&P 500 or Russell 1000 membership. Beta between 0.5 and 2.0 (excludes outlier beta names). NOT reporting earnings within ±1 trading day. Exclude: leveraged ETFs, inverse ETFs, SPACs, closed-end funds, ADRs. Minimum 6-month trading history. Average 15-min bar volume >= 50K shares/bar. Estimated universe: 200-350 names.

### Entry & Exit
ENTRY (evaluated at each bar close, starting bar 3 = 10:00 AM, ending bar 24 = 3:15 PM): Long if: (a) Opening Range direction = LONG (close > BuyLine established at 10:00 AM); (b) OFI z-score current bar > 0; (c) OFI z-score prior bar > −0.5 (persistence); (d) current close > session open; (e) bar realized vol ratio between 0.5× and 2.5× rolling avg; (f) cross-sectional OFI rank = top tercile in universe. Short: mirror image. EXITS — E1 Signal reversal: OFI z-score crosses below −0.5 (long) or above +0.5 (short). E2 Hard time stop: 4 bars (60 min) from entry, hard maximum. E3 Price stop: >0.35% adverse move from entry. E4 Opening range failure: price crosses back through SellLine (from long). E5 Mandatory flat: all positions closed by 15:30 PM. E6 Vol spike: bar realized vol > 3× rolling avg — exit and suspend entries. REBALANCING: Event-driven, not calendar. Expected 1-2 round trips per position per day, most stocks not traded on most days. AVOID: first 2 bars (9:30-10:00 AM opening noise) and last 3 bars (3:30-4:00 PM closing noise). AVOID: within 1 bar of scheduled macro releases (FOMC, NFP, CPI).

Position sizing: signal-weighted by OFI z-score magnitude, capped at ~2× minimum weight. VIX scaling: VIX <18 = 100% target size; VIX 18-25 = 85%; VIX 25-30 = 65%; VIX 30-35 = 50%; VIX >35 = halt new entries. Single-name max: 8-10% of daily gross exposure. Sector cap: 25-30% gross exposure per sector. Daily drawdown halt: stop new entries if P&L < −1.5% NAV. Per-position stop: 0.35% adverse move.
