### Description
A fully intraday, 1-minute bar strategy that exploits three compounding anomalies: (1) intraday time-of-day seasonality (post-open 9:35–10:30 and post-lunch 12:00–13:30 are structurally biased upward per Hirsch 1987 and HMM research); (2) volume-clock adjustment of intraday returns (bars with above-average volume for their time-of-day slot contain more genuine price discovery, while low-volume bars are noise); and (3) cross-sectional sector-relative ranking of volume-adjusted returns within GICS sectors. The strategy constructs a Volume-Adjusted Intraday Return (VAIR) by multiplying each 1-minute return by the bar's Volume Impulse Ratio (current bar volume / rolling 20-session average for that same time-of-day slot, capped at 3×). It then ranks stocks cross-sectionally within sectors by their rolling 30-minute VAIR cumulation score, entering longs in top-quintile relative outperformers and shorts in bottom-quintile relative underperformers during seasonally favorable windows. All positions are closed intraday (30-minute max hold, mandatory flat before 15:35 PM). NO overnight positions. This is entirely distinct from the VSIGM strategy which uses overnight gaps held for 1–5 days.

### Edge
Volume Impulse Ratio (VIR) = V_today(i,s) / V_avg(i,s) over rolling 20 sessions for each same-time slot s. VAIR(i,t) = raw_1min_return(i,t) × min(VIR(i,t), 3.0). Rolling 30-min VAIR_Momentum = cumulative sum of VAIR over 30 bars. Relative Stock Score = VAIR_Momentum(individual) - cap-weighted sector mean VAIR_Momentum. Enter during TOD windows: 9:35–10:30 AM and 12:00–13:30 PM. Long top quintile relative score + positive sector momentum. Short bottom quintile + negative sector momentum. VWAP Gate: price must be within ±0.3% of session VWAP at entry (prevents chasing extended moves).

### Universe
US equities only. Market cap > $5B. 20-day avg daily dollar volume > $100M. 20-day avg daily share volume > 500K. Stock price > $20 (bid-ask spread proxy). S&P 500 or Russell 1000 membership. Minimum 3-month trading history (for 20-session TOD baseline). Exclude: ADRs, SPACs, leveraged ETFs, closed-end funds.

### Entry & Exit
ENTRY (each 1-minute bar during active windows): Long if Relative Stock Score > 80th pctile within sector AND Sector VAIR Momentum > 0 AND price within ±0.3% of VWAP. Short if Relative Stock Score < 20th pctile within sector AND Sector VAIR Momentum < 0 AND price within ±0.3% of VWAP. ACTIVE WINDOWS: 9:35–10:30 AM and 12:00–13:30 PM ET only. EXIT: E1: Relative Stock Score crosses zero. E2: Price deviates > 0.5% from session VWAP in entry direction (position extended). E3: Hard time stop — 30 minutes from entry. E4: Sector VAIR Momentum reverses sign. E5: Mandatory close of all positions by 15:35 PM. INACTIVE PERIODS: 9:30–9:35 (opening volatility), 10:30–12:00 (midday lull), 13:30–15:30 (afternoon drift), 15:30–16:00 (leveraged ETF rebalancing noise).

No overnight positions (hard rule). Single-name max: 10% of daily gross exposure. Sector max: 30% of gross exposure. VIX sizing: VIX < 18 → 100% target size; VIX 18–25 → 75%; VIX 25–35 → 50%; VIX > 35 → halt new entries, close existing. Daily drawdown stop: realized P&L < -1.5% NAV → halt all new entries rest of day. Per-position stop-loss: 0.4% adverse move from entry. ### Description
A quarterly-rebalancing, long/short US equity strategy that decomposes Return on Equity (ROE) into its three DuPont components — Net Profit Margin (NPM), Asset Turnover (AT), and Equity Multiplier (EM) — and exploits the market's tendency to price all ROE improvements equally, regardless of whether the driver is sustainable operational efficiency or transient financial leverage. The core alpha edge: ROE improvements driven by NPM expansion + AT improvement (operational quality) predict persistent future outperformance, while ROE improvements driven primarily by EM expansion (leverage inflation) predict subsequent mean reversion and underperformance. The long book targets companies with expanding operational ROE (margin-driven OR turnover-driven, both confirming directionally) and low/stable financial leverage. The short book targets companies with high ROE levels that are leverage-inflated, declining AT, and elevated EM. A DuPont Quality Score (DQS) separates operational from financial ROE drivers, sector-normalized within GICS groups. The strategy explicitly uses the interaction of all three components as its core signal rather than any single factor — this is what makes it distinct from RACEQ (which uses ROIC trajectory without decomposing operational quality from leverage), CBERM (which uses analyst revisions + accruals), and AQM-52 (52-week high momentum). The bear-steepening yield curve (10Y 4.29%, 30Y 4.91%, Fed Funds 3.64%) amplifies the strategy edge by genuinely penalizing leverage-dependent ROE at the cost of capital level.

Core thesis: The market re-rates operational ROE improvers higher over 6-24 months (Soliman 2008: AT changes have significant estimation power for future profitability growth, Nissim & Penman 2001: AT demonstrates greater persistence than profit margin). Leverage-inflated ROE is exposed when rates stay elevated and refinancing costs rise. Citi thematic research (2026) validates: ROE basket driven by margin expansion + AT improvement outperformed the negative-ROE-trend basket persistently 'despite other factor rotations and macro data shifts.'

### Edge
Three compounding signals via DuPont decomposition: (1) Net Profit Margin Change = [NPM_TTM - NPM_TTM_4Q_ago], sector-neutral z-score. Weight 35%. Captures pricing power + cost discipline. (2) Asset Turnover Change = [AT_TTM - AT_TTM_4Q_ago], sector-neutral z-score. Weight 40%. Primary alpha signal — AT changes show greater persistence (Fairfield & Yohn 2001). Captures genuine operational efficiency improvement: inventory turns, receivables, fixed asset utilization. (3) Leverage Quality Penalty = -[EM_TTM - EM_3Y_avg] / EM_3Y_avg, sector-neutral z-score. Weight 25%. Negative signal: penalizes firms whose ROE improvement is purely EM-expansion (debt-funded). This is the SHORT signal driver.

DQS_Composite = 0.35×z(ΔNPM) + 0.40×z(ΔAT) + 0.25×z(Leverage_Penalty). Long: top quintile DQS AND EM ≤ sector median (quality confirmation). Short: bottom quintile DQS OR high EM_expansion-driven ROE with declining AT.

Secondary confirmation gate: ROE_operational = ROE × (1 - EM_contribution_share). EM_contribution_share = (EM - sector_median_EM) / EM. Longs must have ROE_operational > sector median. Shorts targeted where ROE_total is high but EM_contribution_share > 40% of ROE level.
