### Description
A daily-rebalancing, event-triggered momentum strategy for US equities that exploits opening gaps confirmed by elevated pre-market relative volume. The strategy distinguishes 'informed gaps' (large, volume-confirmed → permanent price shift per Glosten-Milgrom adverse selection theory) from 'noise gaps' (small, low-volume → transient inventory rebalancing → fade). Positions are sized inversely to each stock's 20-day realized volatility (Barroso-Santa-Clara volatility scaling), held for 1–5 trading days to capture the behavioral momentum phase (DHS overconfidence model), and governed by regime filters that key off VIX level, dealer gamma positioning, and rolling gap-continuation hit rates. The strategy was designed for and is currently most potent in the April 2026 macro regime: elevated VIX (~25), negative dealer gamma (gaps extend, not fill), and headline-driven intraday moves that create large, volume-confirmed gap events.

### Edge
Composite Informed Gap Score (IGS) = sign(Gap%) × [0.40×Norm(|Gap%|) + 0.30×Norm(PreVol_Ratio) + 0.20×Norm(3m_Momentum_Rank) + 0.10×Gap_Direction_Match]. Minimum thresholds: |Gap%| ≥ 1.5%, pre-market relative volume ≥ 1.5×. The signal separates informed institutional flow (which continues) from overnight noise (which reverts), confirmed by Glosten-Milgrom (1985) adverse selection decomposition. The negative gamma regime in April 2026 amplifies continuation via dealer delta-hedging mechanics.

### Universe
US equities only. Market cap ≥ $5B. Average daily dollar volume ≥ $50M (20-day). Average daily share volume ≥ 500K shares (20-day). Options open interest ≥ 5,000 contracts. Profitable (ROA > 0%). D/E < 3.0×. Analyst coverage ≥ 3 sell-side analysts. 20-day annualized realized vol between 15% and 80%. Beta to S&P 500 between 0.5 and 2.5. 3-month momentum (skip-1-month) in top 70th pct for longs / bottom 30th pct for shorts. No earnings within ±1 trading day. No M&A announcement.

### Entry & Exit
ENTRY: Daily pre-open screen identifies stocks with |Gap%| ≥ 1.5% AND pre-market relative volume ≥ 1.5× the 20-day average. Rank by IGS composite score. Enter long book (top positive-IGS names) and short book (bottom negative-IGS names) within first 15 minutes after open (9:31–9:45 AM ET) using limit orders within 0.2% of opening print. Apply Dual Thrust opening range confirmation by Day 1 close: BuyLine = Open + 0.5 × 4-day Range proxy. EXIT: E1: trailing stop triggers at 40% of gap-day gain given back from peak. E2: IGS crosses zero on recalculation. E3: 5-trading-day maximum hold. E4: earnings/M&A event. E5: 5-day vol > 3× 20-day vol (exit 50%). E6: VIX > 40 (exit all). E7: 10-signal hit rate < 40% (reduce to 50% sizing).

### Risk Management
Position sizes determined algorithmically: Weight_i ∝ 1/σ_i (20-day realized vol), subject to single-name concentration cap and sector cap (max 35% gross in any GICS sector). Net market exposure bounded within ±50% of gross. Four VIX-driven sizing modes: VIX < 20 → 75% sizing; VIX 20–35 → 100% (current active mode); VIX 35–45 → 50% sizing, long-only; VIX > 45 → exit all. Drawdown rules: 5% drawdown from 20-day high → review; 10% → cut all positions 50%; 15% → exit all, 5-day pause. Passive long vol hedge (VIX calls or tail-risk ETF) at ~3–5% of notional as insurance. Pre-scheduled 50% size reductions on FOMC, CPI, and PPI release days.

### Description
A fully intraday, 1-minute bar strategy that exploits three compounding anomalies: (1) intraday time-of-day seasonality (post-open 9:35–10:30 and post-lunch 12:00–13:30 are structurally biased upward per Hirsch 1987 and HMM research); (2) volume-clock adjustment of intraday returns (bars with above-average volume for their time-of-day slot contain more genuine price discovery, while low-volume bars are noise); and (3) cross-sectional sector-relative ranking of volume-adjusted returns within GICS sectors. The strategy constructs a Volume-Adjusted Intraday Return (VAIR) by multiplying each 1-minute return by the bar's Volume Impulse Ratio (current bar volume / rolling 20-session average for that same time-of-day slot, capped at 3×). It then ranks stocks cross-sectionally within sectors by their rolling 30-minute VAIR cumulation score, entering longs in top-quintile relative outperformers and shorts in bottom-quintile relative underperformers during seasonally favorable windows. All positions are closed intraday (30-minute max hold, mandatory flat before 15:35 PM). NO overnight positions. This is entirely distinct from the VSIGM strategy which uses overnight gaps held for 1–5 days.

### Edge
Volume Impulse Ratio (VIR) = V_today(i,s) / V_avg(i,s) over rolling 20 sessions for each same-time slot s. VAIR(i,t) = raw_1min_return(i,t) × min(VIR(i,t), 3.0). Rolling 30-min VAIR_Momentum = cumulative sum of VAIR over 30 bars. Relative Stock Score = VAIR_Momentum(individual) - cap-weighted sector mean VAIR_Momentum. Enter during TOD windows: 9:35–10:30 AM and 12:00–13:30 PM. Long top quintile relative score + positive sector momentum. Short bottom quintile + negative sector momentum. VWAP Gate: price must be within ±0.3% of session VWAP at entry (prevents chasing extended moves).

Short if Relative Stock Score < 20th pctile within sector AND Sector VAIR Momentum < 0 AND price within ±0.3% of VWAP. ACTIVE WINDOWS: 9:35–10:30 AM and 12:00–13:30 PM ET only. EXIT: E1: Relative Stock Score crosses zero. E2: Price deviates > 0.5% from session VWAP in entry direction (position extended). E3: Hard time stop — 30 minutes from entry. E4: Sector VAIR Momentum reverses sign. E5: Mandatory close of all positions by 15:35 PM. ### Description
A fully intraday, 1-minute bar strategy that harvests transient microstructure noise in large-cap US equities by exploiting the mean-reversion of a stock's beta-adjusted idiosyncratic spread relative to its sector ETF anchor. At each 1-min bar, the strategy computes each stock's excess return above what its rolling sector-ETF beta would predict. These excess returns are cumulated over a 20-bar rolling window to form the idiosyncratic spread X(i,t). The spread is modeled as an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck (OU) process, with parameters estimated pre-session using the prior 20 sessions of 1-min spread history. Trades are triggered by the standardized s-score = (X(i,t) - θ̂) / σ̂_eq where σ̂_eq = σ̂/√(2κ̂). When s-score is extreme (|s| ≥ 1.25), the stock has deviated from its ETF-implied fair path due to transient noise — a structural negative serial correlation confirmed by empirical market microstructure evidence (ρ ≈ -50% at the LOB level). An Internal Bar Strength (IBS) exhaustion filter ((Close-Low)/(High-Low)) at the signal bar confirms buyer/seller fatigue. Half-life gating (10–45 1-min bars) ensures the trade will complete within the session. All positions are closed intraday; no overnight risk. The current macro regime (VIX 22-25, intraday vol at Liberation Day highs per JPMorgan, 2-look vol > close-to-close vol, elevated tariff/geopolitical policy noise) is specifically optimal: JPMorgan explicitly characterized April 2026 intraday moves as 'high-frequency noise' — exactly what SAISR is designed to harvest.

### Description

The strategy identifies stocks in a 'squeeze' state — BBW compressed to near the rolling 20-session minimum for their current time-of-day slot — and enters a directional breakout trade only when the breakout is confirmed by above-average volume (informed order flow) and favorable IBS bar structure (buyer/seller conviction). In the afternoon window (13:00–15:00 PM), a session-cumulative SPX return gate is applied: when SPX is up significantly, LETF rebalancing flows mechanically amplify the upside breakout probability, and vice versa. This is entirely distinct from the existing strategies: VSIGM (daily gap momentum), IVCCM (volume-clock cross-sectional TOD momentum), and SAISR (OU mean-reversion against sector ETF anchor).
